kazi nazrul islam

The national poet of Bangladesh

Rebel Poet

Kazi Nazrul Islam (Bengali: কাজী নজরুল ইসলাম, Bengali pronunciation: [kad͡ʒi nod͡ʒrul islam] (About this soundlisten), 24 May 1899 – 29 August 1976) was a Bengali poet, writer, musician, and the national poet of Bangladesh.[4] Popularly known as Nazrul, he produced a large body of poetry and music with themes that included religious devotion and rebellion against oppression.[5] Nazrul's activism for political and social justice earned him the title of "Bidrohi Kobi" (Rebel Poet).[6] His compositions form the avant-garde music genre of Nazrul Geeti

Awesome Writer

Nazrul's writings explored themes such as freedom, humanity, love, and revolution. He opposed all forms of bigotry and fundamentalism, including religious, caste-based and gender-based.[14] Nazrul wrote short stories, novels, and essays but is best known for his songs and poems. He introduced the ghazal songs in Bengali language[15][16][17][18] and is also known for his extensive use of Arabic and Persian words in his works
